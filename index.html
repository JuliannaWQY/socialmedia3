Hong Kong 2018 Legislative Council By-election Results

Winning by political camps at each of the voting station:


As the map above shows, Hong Kong's two main political power received a pretty even result in the past legislative council election.

Along the coast line of the Hong Kong Island, pro-establishments are winning over the pan-democrats. While at Kowloon side, the two camps divides the battle field evenly.

Up in the New Territory East, though, green triangles, which stand for pan-democrats, are more than pro-establishment's blue squares.

Look into each of the three geographical constituencies for the election results, we can see that pro-establishments only won in Hong Kong Island, while pan-democrats out-numbered their opponents in both Kowloon West and New Territory East in terms of total number of votes.

The two camps had a pretty similiar results except for New Territory East, where pan-democrats' votes was almost two times of pro-establishments.

Major finantial district of the city, including Central, Wan Chai and Admarlty, show higher support to pro-Beijing camp (probably due to Hong Kong's finantial relience to China). At Kowloon and New Territory, where most local Hong Kong people suivive high rent and life expenses, pan-democrats, who are more local-orientated, gain more support.

Sources: hkfp, gov.hk
